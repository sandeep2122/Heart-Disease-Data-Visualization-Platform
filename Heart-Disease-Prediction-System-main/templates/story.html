The Silent Epidemic
Inside India's Heart

A visual story about how lifestyle, geography, and policy shape India's leading cause of death — and what we can do about it.

Chapter 01 · The Scale

Every 33 Seconds,
a Heart Stops in India

Heart disease has surpassed all other causes to become India's #1 killer. With 17.9 million global deaths annually, cardiovascular disease accounts for 32% of all deaths worldwide. In India alone, over 2.8 million people die from heart disease each year — that's one person every 11 seconds.

17.9M

deaths from heart disease globally per year — more than cancer, diabetes, and respiratory disease combined.

💡 The most shocking truth: 80% of these deaths are preventable with early detection, lifestyle changes, and timely medical intervention.

Chapter 02 · The Risk Makers

Lifestyle Choices Are
Writing the Death Sentence

Our data reveals that poor diet, physical inactivity, and smoking together account for 58% of all heart disease risk in the dataset. These are modifiable risk factors — meaning they can be changed. Yet millions continue to live sedentary lives, smoke heavily, and eat poorly.

Patients who exercise daily have only an 11% heart disease rate compared to 72% for those who never exercise. That is a 6.5× difference driven purely by one habit.

🚬 Heavy smokers had a 68% disease rate vs 14% for non-smokers — a 4.9× increase in risk from a single avoidable habit.

Chapter 03 · The Divide

Urban India Burns Faster
Than Rural India

Urban prevalence has nearly doubled since 2015, driven by sedentary desk jobs, fast-food culture, and extreme stress. Rural areas are catching up fast — rising from 10.1% to 18.5% over the decade — as lifestyle habits urbanize without the healthcare infrastructure to match.

Chapter 04 · The Age Trap

Heart Disease is Getting
Younger, Faster

The average age of diagnosis has fallen to 55 years, down 2 years from a decade ago. The 50–69 age band accounts for 60% of all cases, but alarmingly, younger groups (30–49) are showing rapid growth in prevalence.

The message for anyone nearing 40 with risk factors: proactive screening is no longer optional — it's life-saving. High BMI in the 30–49 group is the strongest early warning signal.

📊 41% of the 503 patients in this dataset were diagnosed with heart disease. That's 4 in 10 adults — a proportion that should alarm every household.
