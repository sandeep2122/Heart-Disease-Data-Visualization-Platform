# Step-by-Step Project Documentation: Heart Disease Analysis

**Project Title**: Heart Disease Data Analysis and Visualization
**Prepared By**: Sandeep Kumar & Team (Shristi Kumari, Pranjal Sharma)
**Tools Used**: Python (Pandas, SQLAlchemy), SQLite/MySQL, Tableau, HTML/CSS/Flask

---

## 1. Problem Statement
Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death globally. Identifying the primary risk factors—spanning demographics, clinical conditions, and lifestyle choices—is critical for early intervention. 
**Objective**: To build an interactive, data-driven system that analyzes over 300,000 patient records to visualize the correlations between heart disease and various risk factors (Age, BMI, Smoking, Diabetes, etc.), ultimately embedding these insights into a web application for accessibility by healthcare professionals and policymakers.

## 2. Data Collection (The Dataset)
* **Source**: The 2020 annual CDC survey data (BRFSS - Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System), publicly available on Kaggle/GitHub.
* **Size**: 319,795 rows and 18 columns.
* **Key Features**: `HeartDisease` (Target), `BMI`, `Smoking`, `AlcoholDrinking`, `Stroke`, `PhysicalHealth`, `MentalHealth`, `DiffWalking`, `Sex`, `AgeCategory`, `Race`, `Diabetic`, `PhysicalActivity`, `GenHealth`, `SleepTime`, `Asthma`, `KidneyDisease`, `SkinCancer`.

## 3. Data Cleaning and Preparation (Methodology)
Raw data from public surveys often contains inconsistencies. Our data engineering phase involved the following steps utilizing **Python and Pandas**:
1. **Validation**: Initial analysis confirmed zero missing values (NaNs), but identified 18,078 duplicate rows.
2. **De-duplication**: A Python script (`clean_dataset.py`) was written to drop all duplicate rows, reducing the dataset to a clean 301,717 unique patient records.
3. **Export**: The cleaned data was exported as `heart_disease_tableau_final.csv`.

## 4. Database Integration & Architecting
To ensure our dashboard could handle real-world scale and updates, we migrated from static CSV files to a relational database.
1. **SQLite / MySQL Migration**: We wrote a pipeline (`load_to_mysql.py`) using `SQLAlchemy` to push the 300,000+ records directly into a database schema (`heart_disease_db`).
2. **Monitoring**: Using MySQL Workbench, we monitored the data volume (Table rows, Column count, Memory footprint) to ensure the system was not overloaded prior to connecting Tableau.

## 5. Visualizations & Dashboards (Tableau)
Tableau Desktop was connected directly to the database via ODBC. We engineered 10 specific visualizations and combined them into structured dashboards.

**The 10 Visualizations Created:**
1. **Gender wise Heart Disease**: Bar chart displaying prevalence between Males and Females.
2. **Age wise Heart Disease**: Line/Bar trend showing exponential risk increase after age 60.
3. **People Suffering from Diabetic and Stroke**: Intersection highlighting compounding conditions.
4. **Impact of Smoking and alcohol drinking**: Bar charts proving smoking is a massive accelerant.
5. **Other Diseases vs Stroke**: Overlap analysis of Asthma and Kidney Disease.
6. **Race wise Heart disease**: Demographic distribution.
7. **General Health vs Heart Disease**: Proving self-reported "Poor Health" is highly predictive of CVD.
8. **Physical activity vs heart disease**: Pie/Bar showing the protective nature of exercise.
9. **Age and BMI vs Heart disease**: Scatter plot mapping the physical decline associated with high BMI.
10. **People got stroke suffering from Diabetes and Heart disease**: The "Triple Threat" matrix.

**Dashboard Architecture:**
These sheets were combined into Interactive Dashboards featuring **Data Source Filters** and **Context Filters** to optimize rendering speed, alongside user-facing interactive dropdowns (Age, Race, Sex).

## 6. Web Integration and Publishing
To make the insights accessible:
1. **Publishing**: The final Tableau workbook was deployed live to **Tableau Public**.
2. **Flask Integration**: We extracted the HTML/JS Embed codes generated by Tableau Public.
3. **Deployment**: We injected these embed scripts into our Python Flask application's front-end templates (`dashboard1.html`, `story.html`), replacing static assets with live, interactive data storytelling modules.

## 7. Results and Key Findings
Through this end-to-end data pipeline and visualization strategy, the team derived the following critical insights:
* **The Age Threshold**: Cardiovascular risk scales exponentially after age 60, acting as the primary immutable risk factor.
* **The Vices**: Smoking and Obesity (High BMI) are the two strongest controllable predictors of heart illness in the dataset.
* **The Clinical Cascade**: Heart disease rarely travels alone. The data proves a lethal feedback loop where Diabetes and Heart Disease frequently culminate in a Stroke (The Triple Threat).
* **Actionable Outcome**: Healthcare programs must target smoking cessation and weight management specifically *before* patients reach demographic age milestones, as the compounding clinical risks become significantly harder to reverse later in life.
